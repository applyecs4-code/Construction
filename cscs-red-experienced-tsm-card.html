About the Red Experienced (Technical, Supervisory & Management) CSCS Card

The Red Experienced (Technical, Supervisory & Management) CSCS Card, commonly referred to as the Experienced Worker TSM card, is designed for experienced supervisors, managers and technical professionals in the UK construction industry who are actively pursuing formal qualifications.

This temporary TSM CSCS card validates your on-site experience and confirms your registration for a recognised Construction Technical, Supervisory or Management NVQ or SVQ (Level 3 or above), allowing you to continue working while progressing toward a permanent skilled CSCS card.

Eligibility Requirements for Red Experienced TSM CSCS Card

  • Recent on-site experience in a supervisory, managerial or technical construction role (typically minimum one year within the preceding three years).
  • Current registration for a Construction-related Technical, Supervisory or Management NVQ/SVQ at Level 3 or above qualifying for skilled CSCS card status.
  • Successful completion of the appropriate CITB Health, Safety & Environment Test (Supervisors or Managers & Professionals test as applicable) within the last 24 months.
  • Valid UK identification documentation, such as passport, driving licence, or other government-issued photo ID.
  • CSCS registration confirmation from your training provider detailing: your name, full qualification title and level, and registration date within the last two years.

Validity & Progression After the Red Experienced TSM Card

The Red Experienced Technical, Supervisory & Management CSCS Card is a temporary credential with standard validity of up to three years. This card cannot be renewed upon expiration. Before validity concludes, qualification completion and transition to a permanent skilled card is expected.

Standard Progression Pathways:

  • Gold Supervisor CSCS Card – following completion of recognised Supervisory NVQ/SVQ qualifications.
  • Black Manager CSCS Card – upon achievement of Construction Management-related NVQ/SVQ at appropriate levels.
  • Other relevant skilled or professionally qualified CSCS cards aligned with your specific role and completed qualifications.

Qualification completion and card upgrading strengthens long-term career prospects and demonstrates professional competence for senior positions within UK construction.

Frequently Asked Questions About the Red Experienced TSM CSCS Card

Who should apply for the Red Experienced Technical, Supervisory & Management CSCS Card?

This card is designed for professionals operating in supervisory, managerial or technical capacities on construction sites, possessing recent practical experience, and currently registered for appropriate Construction Technical, Supervisory or Management NVQ/SVQ qualifications at Level 3 or above.

What is the validity period for the Red Experienced TSM CSCS Card?

The Red Experienced Technical, Supervisory & Management CSCS Card is a temporary credential typically valid for maximum three years. As non-renewable, qualification completion and application for permanent skilled or managerial cards must occur before expiration.

Which CITB test is required for this card?

You must successfully complete the appropriate level CITB Health, Safety & Environment Test corresponding to your professional role (Supervisors test or Managers & Professionals test as applicable). Test certification must remain valid within the last two years at application submission.

What registration evidence is necessary?

CSCS documentation from your training provider or awarding body confirming: your full name, complete Technical, Supervisory or Management NVQ/SVQ title and level, and registration date within the preceding two years.

Can the Red Experienced Technical, Supervisory & Management Card be renewed?

No, the Red Experienced TSM card is non-renewable. It serves as temporary validation during qualification completion. Upon qualification achievement, transition to Gold Supervisor, Black Manager or other appropriate skilled CSCS cards is required based on your role and NVQ/SVQ level.
