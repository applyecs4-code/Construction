About the CSCS Red Provisional Card

The CSCS Red Provisional Card is an entry level, temporary CSCS card for people who are new to the UK construction industry. It is designed for new workers, trainees and those who are just starting out on site and need short-term, supervised access.

This provisional CSCS card proves that you have basic awareness of health and safety and that you are allowed to work on UK construction sites under supervision while you build up experience and move towards a permanent CSCS card.

It is widely recognised by UK employers and contractors as the first step towards a long-term construction career and towards cards such as the CSCS Green Labourer Card, Blue Skilled Worker Card, Gold Advanced Craft Card or Black Manager Card.

Why the CSCS Red Provisional Card is Important

UK construction sites operate under strict health and safety regulations. Employers must be confident that even new workers understand basic safety rules before they are allowed on site.

Holding a CSCS Red Provisional Card tells employers that:

  • You have passed the required CITB Health, Safety & Environment (HS&E) Test for workers.
  • You understand basic safety procedures and can follow site rules.
  • You are suitable to work on site under the supervision of experienced staff.

Without a provisional or other valid CSCS card, many UK construction sites will not allow you to start work. This makes the Red Provisional Card a key requirement for anyone who is new to construction and wants to gain experience.

Who Can Apply for a CSCS Red Provisional Card?

The CSCS Red Provisional Card is perfect for people who are:

  • Completely new to the construction industry in the UK.
  • Starting out as apprentices or trainees on building sites.
  • Doing basic or entry-level labouring tasks under close supervision.
  • Working short-term or temporary roles and need quick CSCS card approval.
  • Planning to move onto more advanced CSCS cards once qualified.

If you are not yet ready for a Green Labourer or Blue Skilled Worker Card but need supervised access to site, the Red Provisional Card is the right starting point.

CSCS Red Provisional Card Eligibility Criteria

  • Pass the CITB Health, Safety & Environment (HS&E) Operatives Test within the last 2 years.
  • Provide valid proof of identity such as a UK passport or UK driving licence.
  • Be a new entrant or trainee without another qualified CSCS card.
  • Agree to work under supervision while you gain skills and experience.

Step-by-Step CSCS Red Provisional Card Application Process

  1. Decide whether you need a New, Renewal, or Replacement provisional CSCS card.
  2. Book and pass the CITB HS&E Operatives Test if you have not passed it in the last 2 years.
  3. Gather your ID documents (for example, passport or UK driving licence).
  4. Complete the secure online application form through Apply CSCS UK.
  5. Receive confirmation of your application and document verification.
  6. Get your CSCS Red Provisional Card delivered to your UK address.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who needs a CSCS Red Provisional Card?

New entrants, trainees and apprentices in the UK construction sector who are starting work on site and need temporary, supervised access should hold a CSCS Red Provisional Card or another suitable CSCS card.

How long is the CSCS Red Provisional Card valid for?

In most cases, the CSCS Red Provisional Card is valid for up to 5 years from the date of issue. During this time, you are expected to gain experience and work towards a more permanent CSCS card such as the Green Labourer or Blue Skilled Worker Card.

Can I progress from a Provisional Card?

Yes. Once you have more experience and, ideally, some qualifications, you can move from the Red Provisional Card onto other CSCS cards such as the Green Labourer, Blue Skilled Worker, Gold Advanced Craft or Black Manager Card, depending on your role and NVQ/SVQ level.

How long does it take to receive the card?

Most applicants receive their CSCS Red Provisional Card within 5–6 working days after their documents and application have been successfully verified and approved through Apply CSCS UK.

What documents do I need to apply?

You will usually need proof of identity (such as a passport or UK driving licence) and evidence that you have passed the CITB Health, Safety & Environment (HS&E) Operatives Test within the last 2 years.

Is a CSCS Red Provisional Card mandatory?

On many UK construction sites, a valid CSCS card is required to gain access. For new workers and apprentices without other CSCS cards, the Red Provisional Card is often the most appropriate starting point for supervised work.
