Understanding CSCS Tests

All CSCS Cards require you to take the CITB Health, Safety & Environment Test before applying. This applies whether you are renewing a card or applying for the first time.

You likely need to take the Operatives test, but it's important to check first as different roles require different tests.

Operatives Test

This is the most common test for construction workers. Suitable for:

  • Labourers
  • Bricklayers
  • Carpenters
  • Painters & Decorators
  • Groundworkers
  • And many more...

CSCS Cards Available:

  • Green Labourer CSCS Card
  • Blue Skilled CSCS Card
  • Gold Advanced Craft CSCS Card
  • Red Provisional/Trainee CSCS Cards

Specialist Tests

Some occupations require different tests:

  • Managers → Managers & Professionals Test
  • Supervisors → Supervisor Test
  • Plumbers → Plumbing/Gas Test
  • Demolition Workers → Demolition Test
  • Highways Workers → Highway Works Test
  • Scaffolders/Roofers → Working at Height Test
  • HVACR Trades → Relevant HVACR Test

Key Points to Remember:

  • Your test result is valid for 2 years
  • You must pass the test before applying for your CSCS card
  • Choose the test that matches your job role, not necessarily your qualifications
  • Some specialised roles require specific tests beyond the standard Operatives test
  • If you're progressing to a supervisory or management role, you'll need to take the appropriate test
  • Always check the latest CSCS requirements as they can change

Important UK Legislation

  • Construction (Design and Management) Regulations 2015 (CDM) - Main regulations governing health and safety in construction
  • Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974 - Primary legislation covering occupational health and safety
  • Construction Skills Certification Scheme (CSCS) - Industry competency scheme
  • CITB Health, Safety & Environment Test - Required assessment for most construction roles
