Opening hours

Opening Times – Wick CSCS Test Centre

The Wick HS&E test centre runs pre-booked sessions on selected weekdays. Exact times can vary depending on local demand, but typically:

  • Monday – Friday: test sessions usually between 8:00am and 4:00pm
  • Saturday: occasionally open if additional test days are scheduled
  • Sunday & Bank Holidays: normally closed

Aim to arrive at least 15 minutes before your booked start time. This allows staff to check your ID, store any personal belongings and explain the exam rules. If you arrive late, you may not be allowed to sit the test and the fee can be lost under CITB rules.

Test types

Which CSCS Tests Run at Wick?

The Wick Pearson VUE centre delivers the main CITB HS&E tests linked to CSCS cards:

  • Operatives HS&E Test – required for most Green Labourer and entry-level cards
  • Specialist HS&E Tests – used for many Blue and Gold skilled worker cards
  • Managers & Professionals (MAP) Test – for Black Manager cards, PQP and higher-level roles
  • Retake / renewal HS&E tests – when you need a new pass to renew or upgrade your card

If you're unsure which HS&E test matches your trade, NVQ level or card colour, the ConstructionsCert team can review your details and recommend the correct Wick exam before you book.

Travel & transport

How to Get to the Wick CSCS Test Centre

Wick sits on the north-east coast of mainland Scotland and is reachable by road, rail and local bus. Planning your journey in advance is especially important if you are travelling from rural areas.

By Train

The nearest rail stop is Wick station on the Far North Line. From the station:

  • Walk down towards the town centre following signs for High Street / harbour.
  • Continue towards the river and follow local signs for MacKays Hotel, Union Street.

Walking time from Wick station to MacKays Hotel is typically around 10–15 minutes, depending on pace.

By Bus

Local and regional bus services stop near Wick town centre. From central bus stops, follow directions towards the river, then on to Union Street and MacKays Hotel.

By Car

If you are driving from the south, follow the A9 and then the A99 into Wick. Use postcode KW1 5ED in your sat-nav and follow signs for the town centre and harbour area.

There is no guaranteed on-site parking for candidates at MacKays Hotel. On-street parking and local car parks may be available nearby, but can be limited at busy times. Allow extra time to park and walk to the test centre before your exam.

Booking & ID rules

Booking Your CSCS Test in Wick with ConstructionsCert

CITB has strict photographic ID rules for every HS&E test. At Wick you must bring one original, valid form of photo ID, such as:

  • A current UK or international passport, or
  • A valid UK photocard driving licence

If you arrive at MacKays Hotel without acceptable ID, staff cannot let you sit the exam and your fee may be lost. Always re-check the ID requirements in your confirmation email a few days before your test date.

Results & next steps

After Your CSCS Test at Wick

The HS&E test at Wick is a computer-based exam with 50 multiple-choice questions and a time limit of around 45 minutes. Questions cover site hazards, safe working methods, health risks and your responsibilities under UK health and safety law.

Once you finish, the system marks your answers straight away and reception staff print a score report. This confirms whether you have passed and shows how you performed across the different topic areas.

If you pass, you can usually apply for your CSCS card immediately using the information on your report. If you do not pass this time, ConstructionsCert can help you book another HS&E test at Wick or a nearby centre and point you towards realistic revision options to improve your confidence.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions – CSCS Test Centre in Wick (Caithness)

1. What time should I arrive at the Wick CSCS test centre?

Plan to arrive at MacKays Hotel, 46 Union Street at least 15–20 minutes before your booked start time. This gives you time for ID checks, lockers and a short briefing so you are not rushing into the exam room.

2. Can I change or cancel my Wick HS&E test?

CITB normally requires at least 72 hours' notice to move or cancel a HS&E booking without losing the fee. If you booked through ConstructionsCert and your plans change, contact us as soon as possible so we can explain the options for your Wick appointment.

3. What should I bring on the day?

You must bring your valid photo ID (passport or UK photocard driving licence). It also helps to have your confirmation email or booking reference. Phones, smart watches, notes and other belongings are not allowed in the exam room and must be stored as directed by staff.

4. Is the Wick test centre accessible?

The test room is inside MacKays Hotel. If you require wheelchair access, extra time, a separate room or other reasonable adjustments, tell us when you first enquire so a request can be made to CITB and Pearson VUE before your test is booked.

5. Is this an official CSCS or CITB website?

No. This page is operated by ConstructionsCert, an independent company that helps UK workers understand the process and book CSCS tests and cards. The CSCS scheme is owned by CSCS Ltd, and HS&E tests are delivered on behalf of CITB at Pearson VUE centres such as Wick.
